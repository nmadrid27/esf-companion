# Using ESF with Existing Work

You discovered ESF mid-project. You have notes, drafts, or research already in progress, and you do not want to start over. You do not have to. Here is how to bring existing work into the ESF structure without losing what you have already done.

---

## Step 1: Write a Catch-Up Position Statement

The Position Statement is normally written before AI enters. If you have already been working, write it from where you stand now. The goal is the same: capture your direction in writing before continuing.

A catch-up Position Statement has three parts:

- **Where I started.** What was my original intent for this project? What was I trying to make, argue, or build?
- **Where I am now.** What has shifted? What do I still believe? What has the work changed my thinking about?
- **What I will not compromise.** Looking at the work so far, what must survive in the final version?

This takes ten to fifteen minutes. It is more valuable than it sounds. Writing it down often reveals that your position has drifted during the work — which is exactly what you need to know before continuing.

Use `templates/position-statement.md` as your starting format.

---

## Step 2: Set Up Your Project Folder

Create a project folder and move your existing files in:

```
projects/
└── [your-project-name]/
    ├── position-statement.md       ← Write this first (Step 1)
    ├── records-of-resistance/      ← Create this folder (Step 3)
    ├── ai-use-log.md               ← You will fill this in (Step 4)
    └── work/
        └── [your existing files]  ← Move existing drafts here
```

Keep your work files inside `work/`. The ESF structure wraps around your existing content rather than replacing it.

---

## Step 3: Retroactive Records of Resistance

You did not document your decisions in real time. That is fine. Write a brief retroactive record for any moment you can recall where you redirected the AI:

- A section you rewrote because the AI version was generic
- A framing you rejected because it did not match your intent
- A claim you removed because you could not verify it

A retroactive record looks the same as a real-time one:

```
**AI suggested:** [What you remember the AI proposing]
**I chose instead:** [What you did]
**Why:** [Your reasoning, referenced to your position]
```

You will not remember everything. That is expected. What you can reconstruct is evidence enough. Two or three retroactive records is a reasonable starting point. Going forward, document in real time.

---

## Step 4: Reconstruct Your AI Use Log

If you did not track AI contributions as you went, reconstruct what you can. Look at your work and note:

- Which sections involved AI drafting
- What you asked the AI to do (even roughly)
- What you kept versus what you rewrote significantly

The AI Use Log (`templates/ai-use-log-template.md`, or `templates/ai-use-log-lite-template.md` for a simpler version) does not need to be perfect. An honest approximation is better than nothing and better than no disclosure at all.

---

## Step 5: Continue With the Normal Workflow

Once you have a Position Statement and a basic sense of where AI contributed, continue with the standard ESF process from the Make or Reflect phase:

```
[You are here] ↓
4. MAKE        Work with AI. Log contributions. Record resistance.
               Run the Five Questions at each major section.
       |
5. REFLECT     Compare final work to your Position Statement.
               Write an honest disclosure statement.
```

Run the Five Questions against your existing work before continuing. This is the point where you decide what to keep, what to revise, and what to rebuild.

---

## A Note on Honest Disclosure

When you reach the disclosure stage, note that ESF documentation was started mid-project. You do not need to pretend otherwise. A disclosure that says "ESF practices were applied to the final stages of this project; earlier contributions were reconstructed retrospectively" is more honest than one that implies full documentation from the start.

ESF is not a compliance exercise. The goal is genuine ownership of the work, not a paper trail that looks complete.
